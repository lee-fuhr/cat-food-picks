Combined with added probiotics, it helps soothe sensitive digestive systems. If your cat has shown signs of intolerance to mainstream diets, this is an excellent first try. Our pick: Taste of the Wild Canyon River Feline.

Best for high-protein, active cats

Orijen Original Grain-Free Dry Cat Food packs a dense punch of animal-based protein from fresh and dehydrated meats. Its whole-prey approach mirrors a natural feline diet, making it ideal for energetic cats that need sustained energy. Monitor portions carefully to avoid overfeeding, but for lean, active cats, this is top-tier fuel. Our pick: Orijen Original Grain-Free Dry Cat Food.

Best for budget-friendly feeding

IAMS Proactive Health Dry Cat Food proves that you don’t need to spend a fortune to meet your cat’s basic needs. With chicken as the first ingredient and added taurine, it delivers essential nutrition at an unmatched price. It’s a great everyday staple for households feeding multiple cats or for owners who prioritize cost without sacrificing safety. Our pick: IAMS Proactive Health Dry Cat Food.

Our verdict

For most cat owners, the Taste of the Wild Canyon River Feline offers the best overall value, great nutrition without a premium price tag. If your budget allows and your cat thrives on high-protein fare, the Orijen Original is an excellent upgrade, while the Primal Freeze-Dried Raw is perfect for those committed to a raw diet. The Wellness CORE is a steady runner-up, and the IAMS Proactive Health remains a trustworthy option for cost-conscious households. Whichever you choose, transition slowly and watch your cat’s response, every feline is unique.
